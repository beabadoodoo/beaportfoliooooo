About Hiroshima 広島について

A City of Resilience and Peace

Hiroshima, a city deeply etched in history, has risen from its past to become a symbol of peace, resilience, and hope. Today, it thrives as a vibrant metropolis, offering a profound blend of historical reflection, cultural richness, and modern urban life.

Visitors are often moved by its commitment to promoting peace, evident in its memorial park and museums, while also being charmed by its picturesque landscapes, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality.

Must-Visit Spots 必見スポット

Peace Memorial Park & Museum

The Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park and Museum are solemn reminders of the atomic bombing of 1945. They serve as powerful testaments to the devastating impact of war and Hiroshima's enduring commitment to world peace. It's a deeply moving and essential visit for understanding the city's spirit.

Miyajima (Itsukushima Shrine)

Just a short ferry ride from Hiroshima, Miyajima Island is home to the iconic "floating" torii gate of Itsukushima Shrine. This UNESCO World Heritage site is truly magical, especially at high tide. Explore its tranquil temples, stroll through charming streets, and encounter friendly wild deer.

Hiroshima Castle (Carp Castle)

Originally built in the 1590s, Hiroshima Castle was destroyed by the atomic bomb and faithfully reconstructed. Today, it stands as a beautiful symbol of Hiroshima's resilience, offering a museum inside that details the castle's history and the city's past. The surrounding park is also a lovely place for a stroll.

Local Delights 地元の味覚

Hiroshima's culinary scene is a delight, offering unique flavors that reflect its regional identity. Here are a few must-try dishes!

Okonomiyaki (お好み焼き)

The iconic savory pancake, Hiroshima-style, is characterized by its layers of batter, cabbage, noodles (soba or udon), and various toppings, grilled to perfection.

Tsukemen (つけ麺)

A unique ramen experience where cold noodles are dipped into a rich, flavorful hot broth. Hiroshima's version often features a spicy kick!

Momiji Manju (もみじ饅頭)

A maple-leaf shaped cake, often filled with sweet red bean paste, a popular souvenir and snack from Miyajima and Hiroshima.
